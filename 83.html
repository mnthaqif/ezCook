Menu Name
Tandoori Beef
Summary

Tandoori beef is used extensively with dishes as Tandoori chicken and it is a dish originating in the Indian subcontinent. It is widely popular in South Asia, Malaysia, Singapore, Indonesia, the Middle East and the Western world. It consists of roasted chicken prepared with yogurt and spices. The name comes from the type of cylindrical clay oven, a tandoor, in which the dish is traditionally prepared.

Ingredients

  • Beef meat
  • Tandoori Spice Blend
  • Yogurt Paste
  • onions, tomatoes, thinly sliced (optional)
  • romaine lettuce, cut into bite-size pieces (optional)

    Tandoori Spice Blend Ingredients:

  • 1/2 tablespoon garam masala (store bought)
  • 3/4 teaspoon red chili powder or cayenne pepper (as per taste)
  • 1/2 tablespoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder

    Yogurt Paste:

  • 2 cloves garlic, peeled and minced
  • 1-inch ginger, peeled and minced
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt, lightly whipped
  • 1/4 cup butter, ghee or oil (lightly warmed)
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar or lemon juice
  • 2 teaspoons salt (or as per taste)
  • pinch of red coloring or saffron (optional)

    • Direction

  • 1. In a skillet, lightly toast the Spice Blend ingredients for a quick 2 minutes or until fragrant. In a large bowl, make the Yogurt Paste, add in all the toasted Spice Blend, mix well and set aside.

  • 2. Clean and pat dry the beef meat. With a knife, make 3-4 deep slits on parts of the meat, front and back. Coat chicken with blended Tandoori Spice paste, making sure extra marination goes into the slits of the meat. Marinade for 4-6 hours, preferably overnight.

  • 3. Preheat oven to 375 degree Fahrenheit. Line the baking sheet with aluminum foil and place the broiler rack over it. Place the whole rack in the oven for 10 minutes.

  • 4. Remove rack from the oven, place marinated meat in the oven and bake for 45 minutes. Flip the meat half way, and baste it with extra marinade and continue baking. Reserve juice in the baking sheet, if any and pour juice over the chicken, cool down/refrigerate until ready for grill.

  • 5. Turn broiler oven or grill to High for 5-10 minutes. Cut the whole meat into 8 equal pieces and arrange them onto the grilling rack. Broil for 5-7 minutes on each side until the chicken is lightly charred and completely cooked.

  • 6. Remove from oven, and ready to serve with a bed of fresh salad, Naan bread or Raita.
