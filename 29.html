Menu Name
Oriental Chicken Chop
Summary

Chicken chop is a quintessential Malaysian homegrown ‘western cuisine’. It was developed by the local Chinese of Hainanese origin who had been the cook for the British army and expatriates during the colonial era. They are capable cook who can dish out perfect western cuisine such as lamb chop, mushroom soup and steaks, but they also liked to incorporate the local cooking style into the cuisine. Since most Asians prefer chicken thigh over breast meat, they have developed a variation of ‘chops’ by using the de-boned chicken thigh and serve along with brown sauce or mushroom sauce. Since this concept is similar to prepare pork or lamb chops, it instantly became a favorite dish among the British as well as the locals. This dish has since become the most well-liked western food nowadays in Malaysia. Chicken chops are prepared only by using chicken thigh as it is more tender and juicy, in contrary to breast meat. This also explain why chicken chops remain as the local western food which is relatively less popular in the west..
Ingredients


Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs boneless skinless chicken thighs
  • 2 tablespoons garlic, minced
  • 3 green onions, chopped
  • 6 teaspoons ginger powder
  • 14 cup soy sauce
  • 8 tablespoons chicken broth
  • 3 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 3 teaspoons light soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon Splenda sugar substitute
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch, dissolved in water
    • Direction


    Methods:

  • 1.Marinate chicken thighs in the dark soy sauce for at least 1 hour.

  • 2.Heat oil in a large frying pan over medium heat. Add in garlic and ginger. Saute long enough to allow the ginger and garlic to release their aroma.

  • 3.Add in the chicken and saute until golden brown.

  • 4.Add in green onions, chicken broth, oyster sauce, light soy sauce and Splenda.

  • 5.Stir well.

  • 6.Once the sauce starts to boil, about 3-5 minutes, mix in the cornstarch to thicken the sauce.
