Menu Name
Carbonara Pasta
Summary
Carbonara (Italian: [karbonara]) is an Italian pasta dish from Rome based on eggs, cheese (Pecorino Romano or Parmigiano-Reggiano), and black pepper. Spaghetti is usually used as the pasta; however, fettuccine, rigatoni, linguine or bucatini can also be used. The dish was created in the middle of the 20th century
Ingredients
  • 3 large free-range egg yolks
  • 40g Parmesan cheese, plus extra to serve sea salt
  • freshly ground black pepper
  • 200g spaghetti
  • 1 clove of garlic, peeled
  • extra virgin olive oil

    • Direction
  • 1.Put the egg yolks into a bowl, finely grate in the Parmesan, season with pepper, then mix well with a fork and put to one side.

  • 2.Cook the spaghetti in a large pan of boiling salted water until al dente. if you have any, all over the base of a medium frying pan (this will add fantastic flavour, or use 1 tablespoon of oil instead), then place over a medium-high heat. Crush the garlic clove with the palm of your hand, add it to the pan and leave it to flavour the fat for a minute. then cook for 4 minutes, or until it starts to crisp up.

  • 3.Pick out and discard the garlic from the pan, then, reserving some of the cooking water, drain and add the spaghetti. Toss well over the heat so it really soaks up all that lovely flavour, then remove the pan from the heat. Add a splash of the cooking water and toss well, season with pepper, then pour in the egg mixture – the pan will help to cook the egg gently, rather than scrambling it. Toss well, adding more cooking water until it’s lovely and glossy. Serve with a grating of Parmesan and an extra twist of pepper.
