Menu Name
Creamy Seafood Marinara Pasta
Summary
Marinara (English: mariner's) sauce is an Italian sauce that originated in Naples, usually made with tomatoes, garlic, herbs, and onions.Its many variations can include the addition of capers, olives and spices.It is occasionally sweetened with a dash of red wine. This sauce is widely used in Italian-American cuisine, which has greatly diverged from its Old World origins. Italians refer to marinara sauce only in association with other recipes. For instance, spaghetti alla marinara literally translates to "spaghetti mariner's style" (from the adjective marinara with the feminine suffix -a pertaining to salsa, Italian for sauce), but tomato sauce alone in Italy is called sugo/salsa al/di pomodoro or pummarola
Ingredients
  • San Remo Reg No15 Wide Fettuccine 500g
  • 750g seafood marinara mix
  • 2 tbsp butter or oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed salt, pepper to taste
  • ½ cup dry white wine
  • 300ml thickened cream
  • squeeze of lemon juice
  • chopped parsley
  • 1 tbsp cornflour
  • ¼ cup water

    		•
    Direction
  • 1.Heat butter in large frying pan Add garlic, cook for 1 minute stirring constantly.

  • 2.Mix in wine and cream, bring to gentle boil whilst stirring.

  • 3.Cook for 3 minutes, mix cornflour with water and add to cream mixture. Stir constantly until mixture boils and thickens.

  • 4.Add lemon juice, salt, pepper and seafood. Gently cook for 2 to 3 minutes, add parsley.Cook fettuccine according to packet directions.

  • 5.Drain and mix with half the seafood sauce. Serve with the remaining sauce spooned over each serve.
