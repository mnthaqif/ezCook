Menu Name
Nasi Kandar
Summary

Nasi kandar is a popular northern Malaysian dish, which originates from Penang. It is a meal of steamed rice which can be plain or mildly flavoured, and served with a variety of curries and side dishes

Ingredients

  • 1/2 kg chicken cut into pieces,
  • a pinch of turmeric powder and Oil( as needed) for deep frying.
  • 2 tomatoes quartered
  • 1 pandan leaf
  • 1-2 sprigs of curry leaves
  • 1 inch of cinnamon stick
  • 3 anise star
  • 3 cardamoms
  • 2 onions thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup dark soya sauce
  • 1/4 cup tomato sauce/ puree
  • 2 tbsp palm sugar
  • 1/2 cup oil
  • a pack of beef curry powder.
  • 4 cloves of garlic
  • onion
  • ginger
  • poppy seeds ( khus khus)
  • curry powder

    • Direction

  • 1.Clean the chicken and coat it with turmeric and salt ( this will ensure that all the micro-germs are disinfected naturally).Leave the marinade for 30 miniutes and deep fry the chicken till cooked and golden brown.

  • 2.Heat up oil and saute the onions until they turn brown, add the laksa leaf, curry leaves, cinnamon, anise star and cardamom and fry until fragrant.

  • 3.Add in the ground curry paste and constantly stir until the mixture is well incorporated. Add some water and simmer for 10-12 minutes.

  • 4.Now add the soya sauce, tomato sauce/ puree, sugar, and salt to taste.

  • 5.Let the gravy thicken for a while and then you add in the fried chicken. Simmer until it condenses, add the tomato and garnish it with coriander leaves.

  • Enjoy the Nasi Kandar with hot steaming rice.
