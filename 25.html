Menu Name
Grilled Chicken Chop
Summary

Chicken chop is a quintessential Malaysian homegrown ‘western cuisine’. It was developed by the local Chinese of Hainanese origin who had been the cook for the British army and expatriates during the colonial era. They are capable cook who can dish out perfect western cuisine such as lamb chop, mushroom soup and steaks, but they also liked to incorporate the local cooking style into the cuisine. Since most Asians prefer chicken thigh over breast meat, they have developed a variation of ‘chops’ by using the de-boned chicken thigh and serve along with brown sauce or mushroom sauce. Since this concept is similar to prepare pork or lamb chops, it instantly became a favorite dish among the British as well as the locals. This dish has since become the most well-liked western food nowadays in Malaysia. Chicken chops are prepared only by using chicken thigh as it is more tender and juicy, in contrary to breast meat. This also explain why chicken chops remain as the local western food which is relatively less popular in the west.There are many variations to prepare chicken chops. It can be a breaded chicken chop with black pepper sauce, pan-fried or grilled to perfection and served with brown sauce or mushroom sauce. There is even chicken chop drizzled with sweet and spicy sauce served in one of the local coffee chains. The common side dishes served along with the chicken chop are coleslaw and French fries or mashed potatoes. Some restaurants also served along with garlic bread and salad.
Ingredients


Ingredients:

Two pieces of thigh + drumstick, cut in chicken chop style

  • 2 Tbsp oyster sauce
  • 2 Tbsp dark soy sauce
  • 1 Tbsp oil Pepper and some rosemary (optional)
  • 1 small pack king oyster mushrooms
  • 1 yellow onion
  • ¼ cauliflower

    • *Mushrooms and cauliflower can be substituted with other items. Carrot, leek, capsicum, and tomato are all great choices
    Direction


    Method:

  • 1. Marinate the chicken with oyster sauce, dark soy sauce and pepper. Feel free to add some rosemary too. Let this sit for at least 15-20 minutes.

  • 2. Heat up the grill pan, and pan-fry the chicken with a little oil for 1-2 minutes on each side.

  • 3. Place the mushrooms and vegetables around the chicken and shove everything into a the oven preheated to 175°C.

  • 4. Bake for 20 minutes, remove and sprinkle some salt on the vegetables. Serve hot.
